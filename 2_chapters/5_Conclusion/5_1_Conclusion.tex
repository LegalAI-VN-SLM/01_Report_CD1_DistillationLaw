\section{Conclusion}

This thesis investigated whether the legal reasoning ability of a large, reinforcement-learning-aligned teacher can be transferred into lightweight Small Language Models (SLMs) suitable for secure, on-premise deployment. We designed and validated a multi-phase distillation framework---Offline Logit Distillation, On-Policy Knowledge Distillation, and Diagnosis-Driven Preference Optimization---on Qwen3 students at the 0.6 and 1.7 billion parameter scales across Vietnamese legal Natural Language Inference (NLI), Multiple-Choice (MCQ), and Syllogism tasks. The principal findings are summarized below.

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{A multi-phase framework was realized and validated.} From a shared SFT base, two independent tracks branch in parallel: an offline track in which offline distillation \parencite{hinton2015distillingknowledgeneuralnetwork} is followed by DPO alignment \parencite{rafailov2024directpreferenceoptimizationlanguage} on top of the offline checkpoint, and an on-policy track in which on-policy distillation \parencite{agarwal2024onpolicydistillationlanguagemodels} is applied directly to the base. The 1.7B model after DPO reached the strongest in-distribution NLI Accuracy (0.8156) and Syllogism ROUGE-L (0.5684), at an Overall of 0.5280---marginally below the offline checkpoint (0.5322), reflecting a task-profile trade-off rather than a uniform gain.
    \item \textbf{Quantized adaptation is essential at small scale.} On the 0.6B student, QLoRA acted as an implicit regularizer: its cross-entropy loss settled low ($0.64 \rightarrow 0.37$) and stayed consistently below LoRA's, which remained elevated (around $0.56$), preventing catastrophic forgetting and raising the Syllogism ROUGE-L by roughly 33\%.
    \item \textbf{On-policy distillation mitigates exposure bias.} It improved the 1.7B NLI Accuracy from 0.7305 to 0.7870 in-distribution and achieved the highest NLI Accuracy (0.920) and Syllogism ROUGE-L (0.372) among all distilled models on the out-of-distribution VLSP2025 Public-Test.
    \item \textbf{LLM-based diagnosis produces cleaner alignment data.} Replacing rule-based diagnosis with an LLM-as-a-Judge raised the rate of genuinely acceptable rollouts from 11.1\% to 20.7\% and improved detection of flawed reasoning from 40.5\% to 63.5\%, strengthening the DPO preference set.
    \item \textbf{Model scale is decisive.} The 1.7B student consistently outperformed the 0.6B by a factor of $1.5$--$2\times$, and the benefits of on-policy distillation and DPO emerged only above a minimum capacity threshold, destabilizing the 0.6B model.
\end{enumerate}

Overall, a carefully staged pipeline anchored by quantized adaptation and semantically-grounded preference diagnosis endows a compact 1.7B legal SLM with strong reasoning on in-domain NLI and syllogistic tasks, where it surpasses the supervised baseline. The baseline, however, retains an edge on multiple-choice recall and on out-of-distribution data, exposing a specialization-versus-generalization trade-off that we examine in the following section.
