\section{Limitations}

Several limitations qualify the scope of our conclusions and motivate the directions in the next section.

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Capacity-induced instability at 0.6B.} On-policy distillation and DPO were counterproductive for the smallest student, whose Overall score fell to 0.3104 and 0.1861 respectively, as the preference penalty and rollout noise exceed the absorptive capacity of a sub-billion-parameter network.
    \item \textbf{Over-specialization on out-of-distribution data.} On the VLSP2025 Public-Test the 1.7B SFT baseline scored highest (0.6395), indicating that distillation over-specializes to the training distribution---most visibly on MCQ, where every distilled model scored below 0.56 against the baseline's 0.74.
    \item \textbf{Proxy and composite metrics.} The Syllogism task uses ROUGE-L as a proxy rather than the official LLM-as-a-Judge scoring, and the Overall score is an unweighted mean mixing accuracy with ROUGE-L; both are coarse, so the per-task metrics remain the primary basis for comparison.
    \item \textbf{Single-run evaluation.} Each configuration was trained and evaluated once, without multiple seeds or confidence intervals on a 325-sample test set, so small gaps (e.g., 0.5322 vs.\ 0.5280) may fall within noise and our comparisons should be read as indicative trends.
    \item \textbf{Narrow experimental scope.} A single 2,603-sample corpus, one legal domain (Vietnamese statutory law), and one teacher (\texttt{Qwen3-4B-legal-GRPO}) bound the attainable performance.
    \item \textbf{Potential answer leakage from the oracle teacher.} The teacher is conditioned on the gold answer during on-policy training; although the loss acts on the student's own trajectory with KL clipping, we did not ablate how much gain reflects genuine reasoning transfer versus residual verdict leakage.
    \item \textbf{External dependence and inference-scoped privacy.} The preference data relies on an external API (\texttt{GPT-4o-mini}), so the privacy guarantee applies only to inference and on-premise deployment---not to training---and reproducibility is limited.
    \item \textbf{Hardware constraints.} Limited compute precluded evaluating larger students (4B and beyond), and the on-policy phase ran roughly eight times slower than offline distillation.
\end{enumerate}
