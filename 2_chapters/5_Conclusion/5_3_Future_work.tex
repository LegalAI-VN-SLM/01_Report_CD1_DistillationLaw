\section{Future work}

Building on the limitations above, we outline four directions for future research.

\paragraph{Resolving catastrophic forgetting.}
QLoRA only mitigates forgetting; the 0.6B student still collapses and distilled models lose general competence on unseen data. Continual-learning remedies---rehearsal-based replay of SFT data, parameter regularization (Elastic Weight Consolidation, L2-SP, or a KL anchor to the SFT policy), and adapter merging---could retain both foundational generalization and specialized distilled knowledge.

\paragraph{Capacity-aware alignment for small models.}
To prevent the 0.6B collapse under DPO, future work could adopt a curriculum of gradually increasing preference difficulty or replace DPO with gentler objectives such as KTO, ORPO, or IPO, enabling sub-billion-parameter models to benefit from preference alignment without destabilizing their reasoning.

\paragraph{Counteracting over-specialization.}
Mixing general-domain data and replay buffers during distillation, and penalizing divergence from the broadly-capable SFT policy, could narrow the specialist-versus-generalist gap observed on the out-of-distribution benchmark.

\paragraph{Scaling the student and diversifying the teacher.}
Distilling into larger students (4B and beyond), adopting multi-teacher or self-distillation to aggregate complementary signals, and expanding the data volume and range of legal sub-domains would raise the performance ceiling imposed by a single teacher and limited compute.
