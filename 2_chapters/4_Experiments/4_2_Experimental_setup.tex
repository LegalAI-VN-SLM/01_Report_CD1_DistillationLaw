\section{Experimental Setup}

To validate the efficiency of the proposed 3-phase distillation and alignment framework, we establish a standardized experimental environment. In the following subsections, we describe the specific architectural parameters of the teacher and student models, the hyperparameter configuration space utilized across each optimization phase, the hardware and software systems employed, and the key debugging corrections implemented to ensure numerical stability and generalization.

\subsection{Teacher and Student Models}

We utilize a pre-aligned, instruct-tuned model as our teacher optimization oracle: Qwen3-4B-legal-GRPO (accessible on Hugging Face as thangvip/qwen3-4b-vietnamese-legal-grpo). This model was pre-trained on Vietnamese legal statutes and aligned via Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO) to generate structured reasoning chains. To operate within a constrained hardware setup, the teacher model is loaded in 4-bit NormalFloat (NF4) quantized format during the distillation phases, allowing it to serve as a frozen target while maintaining a low VRAM footprint.

We evaluate two distinct student architectures to analyze the impact of model parameter scale on legal reasoning:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Qwen3-0.6B-SFT:} A lightweight student model containing 0.6 billion parameters, initialized from the supervised fine-tuning checkpoint (either the full-parameter HoangVuSnape/qwen3-0.6b-sft or the quantized adapter version HoangVuSnape/qwen3-0.6b-qlora-sft) and trained using Quantized Low-Rank Adaptation (QLoRA) adapters.
    \item \textbf{Qwen3-1.7B-SFT:} A moderately sized student model containing 1.7 billion parameters, initialized from the supervised fine-tuning checkpoint HoangVuSnape/qwen3-1.7b-sft and fine-tuned using standard Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA) adapters.
\end{enumerate}

Both student models are initialized from their respective supervised fine-tuning (SFT) checkpoints to ensure they possess baseline legal query-answering capabilities before the logit distillation process begins.

\subsection{Configuration Space and Hyperparameters}

Each of the three phases is configured with a conservative hyperparameter setting tailored to its objective. Both refinement tracks---Phase 2 (on-policy) and the Phase 1$\rightarrow$Phase 3 offline track---start from the same supervised fine-tuned (SFT) base. The complete configuration is summarized in Table~\ref{tab:hparams}.

Several choices warrant explanation. Across all phases we keep the learning rate low---down to $5.0 \times 10^{-6}$ for DPO---to prevent catastrophic forgetting of the distilled knowledge. In Phase 1 the weight $\alpha = 0.3$ places $70\%$ of the loss on the hard-label cross-entropy anchor and $30\%$ on the KL-divergence term, while the top-$K=50$ sparse logit representation reduces the storage and computation of the teacher targets. In Phase 2 the teacher operates as an oracle with privileged access to the gold answer, the student samples its own rollouts at temperature $0.7$ with nucleus sampling $p=0.9$, and KL clipping at $C=5.0$ restricts the gradient influence of formatting tags. Phase 3 uses the \texttt{adamw\_8bit} optimizer to conserve VRAM and takes the Phase 1 checkpoint as the DPO reference policy.

\subsection{Hardware and Framework}

Due to varying memory requirements across the training phases, execution was divided between two hardware environments:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Kaggle Multi-GPU Environment (2x NVIDIA Tesla T4 16GB VRAM):} Used for Phase 1 Offline KD, Phase 3 DPO, and the training of the 0.6B student model during Phase 2 On-Policy KD. To support On-Policy training within this VRAM constraint, we implement a \textit{Model Split} strategy: the trainable student model is loaded onto GPU 0 (\texttt{cuda:0}), while the teacher oracle is loaded in frozen 4-bit mode on GPU 1 (\texttt{cuda:1}). Student rollouts are generated on GPU 0 and transferred to GPU 1 for logit calculation, and the resulting teacher logits are transferred back to GPU 0 to calculate the loss and run backpropagation.
    \item \textbf{Dedicated Docker Environment (1x NVIDIA RTX 6000 Ada 48GB VRAM):} Utilized for the training of the larger 1.7B student model during Phase 2 On-Policy KD. The 48GB VRAM capacity allows both the teacher and student models to reside on the same device, supporting a larger batch size (batch size of 8) and reducing training time from 10 hours to 5 hours.
\end{enumerate}

The software frameworks utilized include: (i) Unsloth for accelerating parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) and reducing memory utilization, (ii) TRL (Transformer Reinforcement Learning) for training via the \texttt{DPOTrainer} class, and (iii) bitsandbytes for quantized model loading and 8-bit optimizer implementations.
