\chapter*{KNOWLEDGE DISTILLATION FOR VIETNAMESE LEGAL REASONING IN SMALL LANGUAGE MODELS\\ABSTRACT}

Deploying commercial Large Language Models (LLMs) in the legal domain is limited by data privacy, cost, and latency, while compact Small Language Models (SLMs) lack multi-step reasoning capabilities. This study addresses this gap by proposing a hybrid knowledge distillation (KD) and preference optimization framework that transfers legal reasoning from a reinforcement-learning-aligned teacher into Vietnamese legal SLMs at the 0.6B and 1.7B scales.

From a shared supervised fine-tuned (SFT) base, the framework branches into two parallel tracks: an on-policy track (Phase 2) mitigating exposure bias via student rollouts, and an offline track combining Offline Logit Distillation (Phase 1) with Diagnosis-Driven Direct Preference Optimization (Phase 3) using an LLM-as-a-Judge. 

Evaluated on three Vietnamese legal tasks, the 1.7B distilled student achieves the strongest in-distribution reasoning on NLI (0.8156) and syllogism (0.5684) tasks. Quantized adaptation (QLoRA) acts as an implicit regularizer, preventing catastrophic forgetting on the 0.6B student, though advanced alignment phases destabilize this sub-billion scale. While the SFT baseline generalizes better on out-of-distribution data, our framework demonstrates the feasibility of secure, compact legal reasoning assistants for local deployment.
