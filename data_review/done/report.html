Distillation Pipeline

The pipeline is divided into 4 modules: Loader (load model), Generator (run inference), Parser (extract + quality gates), and Pipeline (orchestrate everything).

Critical rule: Split data BEFORE distillation

If all 3,428 records go through the Teacher before train/test splitting, the Student will suffer data leakage. Split 80/10/10 first, only distill the train portion.

raw_unified.jsonl (3,428 records) | v split_train_val_test.py (stratify by task_type) | split_train_v1.jsonl (80% ~ 2,742) ← only this gets distilled split_val_v1.jsonl (10% ~ 343) split_test_v1.jsonl (10% ~ 343) ← NEVER shown to Teacher | v chunked_distill.py -> Teacher inference -> parser.py | distilled_train_clean.jsonl (clean samples -> train Student) distilled_train_rejected.jsonl (bad samples -> debug)

Three Root Causes

The problem is not the Teacher — it's the Data Schema and Prompt Objective

This was the most important discovery of the project

Root Cause #1: Broken NLI Schema from Converter

The file converter to_jsonl.py produced duplicate choices and hard-coded incorrect label_semantics.

Duplicate choices:
A. "not relevant" B. "not relevant and cannot..." C. "not relevant" ← duplicate of A D. "relevant"
Consequence:

The Student learns a shortcut: "if 3 options look the same, pick the remaining one" instead of learning true semantic relevance.

label_semantics maps "0=relevant" but choices[0] is "not relevant" → contradiction.

Root Cause #2: English Reasoning

SeaLLMs-v3 is a multilingual model. When the prompt is not strict enough, it automatically switches to English:

"teacher_reasoning": "The legal document provided is directly related to the question about endorsement transfer of promissory notes..."

For a Vietnamese legal SLM, the Student will learn a bilingual response style — completely undesirable.

Root Cause #3: Wrong Syllogism Objective in Prompt

The old prompt asked Teacher: "Is this question answerable?"

Teacher answered:

"answerable"

Classification, not QA

→
Should have answered:

"No more than 10 days from complete application; if verification needed, no more than 45 days"

Actual legal content

Lesson learned: teacher_valid = true only means the parser successfully parsed JSON — it does not mean the sample has good quality. More quality gate layers are needed.

Part II

Continual Pre-Training

Injecting Vietnamese legal knowledge into a Small Language Model

08

Why CPT?

SLMs like Qwen, Gemma at 1B-3B scale are strong at general language understanding. But when asked about Vietnamese law — law names, decree numbers — they answer vaguely or incorrectly.

Continual Pre-Training (CPT) teaches the model about the legal domain without losing general knowledge. Using next-token prediction objective — the model reads legal texts and learns to predict the next token.

Goal: Add legal domain knowledge — vocabulary, document structure, and Vietnamese legal expertise — before SFT.

24

Cross-Analysis & Recommendations

Key Issues

  • MC accuracy very low (0.16) — 64% of answers could not be extracted
  • NLI unknown ~11%, accuracy on extractable answers ~0.768
  • Thinking flag is a data attribute, not inference mode — interpret with caution

Recommendations

  • Fix MC/NLI parser: constrained decoding or regex extraction
  • Use direct mode (thinking off) for inference — outperforms thinking on
  • Add more data or re-weight for MC task
  • Add eval_strategy=steps for more granular monitoring

Full Pipeline Overview

VLSP 2023: 3,428 legal questions | v Knowledge Distillation (Gold-Conditioned) - Teacher: CMC-AI-Legal-32B / SeaLLMs-v3 - 9 Quality Gates | v VLSP 2025: 7,958 legal documents (~46M tokens) | v Continual Pre-Training (Qwen3-4B) - LoRA r=64 | QLoRA 4-bit NF4 - 1 epoch, PPL: 1.83-1.86 | v Supervised Fine-Tuning (Qwen3-1.7B-legal-pretrain) - QLoRA r=16, 3 epochs - NLI: 68% acc | MC: 16% acc | Syllo: 0.39 ROUGE-L | v Next: Fix MC parser, more epochs, thinking mode tuning

From 3,428 raw questions and 7,958 legal documents, the pipeline built a model capable of understanding Vietnamese legal language. NLI and Syllogism show promising results. MC needs parser fixes before proper evaluation. The journey is long, but the foundation is solid.
